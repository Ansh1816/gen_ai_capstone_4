# Attendance Management Protocol v3.2

## Purpose
This protocol establishes standardized procedures for managing patient appointment attendance in outpatient healthcare facilities. The goal is to reduce no-show rates, optimize scheduling efficiency, and ensure continuity of care.

## Risk Stratification Framework

### Low Risk (No-Show Probability < 30%)
- **Standard Confirmation**: Automated appointment reminder dispatched 48 hours before the scheduled appointment.
- **Channel**: SMS or email based on patient communication preference.
- **No additional intervention required** unless patient history indicates recurring issues.
- **Documentation**: Log automated confirmation in the Patient Contact Register.

### Medium Risk (No-Show Probability 30%–55%)
- **Enhanced Reminder Protocol**: Send a personalized SMS reminder 24 hours before appointment in addition to the standard 48-hour reminder.
- **Secondary Contact**: If the patient does not acknowledge the SMS, initiate a brief courtesy call to confirm attendance.
- **Staff Assignment**: Front-desk staff should flag medium-risk appointments for day-of follow-up.
- **Flexible Rescheduling**: Proactively offer alternative appointment times or telehealth options.

### High Risk (No-Show Probability > 55%)
- **Direct Outreach Required**: A staff member must place a phone call to the patient at least 24 hours before the appointment.
- **Confirmation Deadline**: If no confirmation is received within 4 hours of appointment time, the slot may be reallocated.
- **Overbooking Authorization**: High-risk slots may be double-booked with standby patients per the Overbooking Guideline.
- **Care Coordination**: If the patient has chronic conditions, escalate to the care coordinator for welfare check.
- **Documentation**: All outreach attempts must be logged with timestamps in the Patient Contact Register.

## Key Performance Indicators
- Target no-show rate: Below 10% facility-wide
- Patient contact rate for high-risk: 95% within 24 hours
- Same-day slot utilization: Above 92%
