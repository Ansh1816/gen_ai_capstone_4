# Patient Engagement and No-Show Reduction Strategies

## Purpose
This document outlines evidence-based strategies for improving patient engagement and reducing appointment no-shows. These strategies are informed by published healthcare operations research and best practices.

## Understanding No-Show Risk Factors

### Demographic Factors
- **Age**: Younger patients (18–30) show significantly higher no-show rates. Engagement strategies should be targeted appropriately.
- **Socioeconomic Status**: Patients on government assistance programs (scholarships/subsidies) may face transportation or financial barriers.
- **Gender**: Studies show minor differences in no-show rates by gender, but these should not be the primary factor in intervention design.

### Appointment Factors
- **Lead Time (Awaiting Time)**: Longer gaps between scheduling and appointment date correlate with higher no-show rates. Appointments booked more than 14 days in advance are at elevated risk.
- **Previous No-Shows**: The strongest predictor of future no-shows. Patients with 2+ historical no-shows require intensive engagement.
- **Time of Day**: Late afternoon appointments tend to have higher no-show rates.
- **Day of Week**: Monday and Friday appointments may have slightly elevated risk.

### Clinical Factors
- **Chronic Conditions**: Patients with hypertension, diabetes, or disabilities may have complex care needs that affect attendance. However, chronic condition patients often have LOWER no-show rates due to care urgency.
- **SMS Reminders**: While helpful, SMS alone reduces no-shows by only 5–10%. Multi-channel reminders are more effective.

## Evidence-Based Intervention Strategies

### Tier 1: Standard (Low Risk)
- Automated appointment confirmation via preferred channel
- Patient portal access for self-service rescheduling
- Clear pre-appointment instructions

### Tier 2: Enhanced (Medium Risk)
- Personalized reminder messages mentioning specific care benefits
- Offer of telehealth alternatives when appropriate
- Transportation assistance information
- Flexible rescheduling options with multiple time slots
- Brief motivational messaging emphasizing health benefits of attendance

### Tier 3: Intensive (High Risk)
- Direct phone call from care coordinator or nurse
- Address specific barriers (transportation, work schedule, childcare)
- Offer same-day or walk-in alternatives
- Coordinate with social services if socioeconomic barriers identified
- Consider home visits for vulnerable populations
- Implement patient contracts for recurring no-show patients
- Welfare check protocol for patients with chronic conditions who miss appointments

## Transportation and Access Barriers
- Provide information about public transportation routes to clinic
- Partner with rideshare services for subsidized medical transportation
- Offer telehealth/virtual visits as alternative to in-person appointments
- Consider satellite clinic options closer to patient populations

## Measuring Success
- Track no-show rate by intervention tier
- Monitor patient satisfaction after intervention
- Calculate cost savings from reduced no-shows
- Report on appointment slot utilization improvements
- Review strategy effectiveness quarterly and adjust thresholds
